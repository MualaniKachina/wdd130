History

Rafting, particularly whitewater rafting, has a rich history rooted in exploration and evolving into a popular recreational sport.

Early attempts to navigate rivers, like the Snake River, date back to the early 1800s. The development of inflatable rafts, like the one created by John Fremont in 1843, was crucial for navigating rough waters. Commercial whitewater rafting trips emerged in the mid-20th century, and the sport gained further recognition with its inclusion in the Olympic Games in 1972.
